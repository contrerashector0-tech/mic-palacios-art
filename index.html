The Artist

Where shadow meets art

"Art is how I speak what words can't hold — the shadows, the memories, the beauty in what's unseen."

Michelle Palacios is a self-taught traditional artist from El Paso, Texas — a border city where Mexican cultural heritage and American art run deep. Under the banner of Dark Shadows Art Studio, her work explores the space where gothic shadow meets vibrant Chicana tradition.

Drawing on El Paso's rich Día de los Muertos culture, her paintings breathe life into calaveras, sugar skulls, and ceremonial florals — rendered in dark, high-contrast compositions that honor the dead while celebrating the living. Her pop culture fan art brings beloved characters into a gothic sensibility, and her custom commission work captures subjects with emotional depth and detailed craftsmanship.

Michelle's work has been displayed at El Paso's Finest gallery in the Downtown Arts District, and she is a regular vendor at the El Paso MCAD Día de los Muertos Festival and El Paso Comic Con — bringing her art directly to the community that inspires it.
